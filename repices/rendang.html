Rendang (Indonesian Spiced Beef Stew)

Rendang is a rich and flavorful Indonesian dish made from tender beef slowly cooked in coconut milk and a blend of aromatic spices such as lemongrass, galangal, garlic, turmeric, ginger, and chili. Originating from the Minangkabau ethnic group of West Sumatra, rendang is known for its deep, complex flavor and melt-in-the-mouth texture. Traditionally served on special occasions, rendang is now enjoyed worldwide as one of Indonesia's most iconic and beloved dishes.

Ingredients :

  • 1 kg beef (cut into chunks)
  • 1 liter thick coconut milk (from 3 grated coconuts – first pressing, without added water)
  • 550 grams grated coconut, toasted until golden brown
  • 5 Indonesian bay leaves (daun salam)
  • 1 turmeric leaf (daun kunyit)
  • 10 kaffir lime leaves (daun jeruk)
  • 5 stalks lemongrass, bruised
  • 1/2 stick cinnamon
  • 3 cloves
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 star anise

Ground spices (to blend):

  • 65 grams garlic
  • 125 grams shallots
  • 15 grams fresh turmeric
  • 35 grams ginger
  • 75 grams galangal
  • 35 grams candlenuts (kemiri)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1 cardamom pod
  • 1/4 whole nutmeg

    Saut√© the ground spices and toasted grated coconut in a pan. Stir well until fragrant.
  2. Step 2 :
    Add the bay leaves, kaffir lime leaves, turmeric leaf, and bruised lemongrass. Cook until aromatic.
  3. Step 3 :
    Add the beef chunks to the pan. Stir until the beef is evenly coated with the spices.
  4. Step 4 :
    APour in the thick coconut milk. Mix everything well.
  5. Step 5 :
    Simmer the rendang over low heat, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and dries out. This process may take several hours to achieve the perfect tender texture and rich flavor.
  6. Step 6 :
    Once the rendang is dry and the beef is tender, it's ready to be served for special occasions.
