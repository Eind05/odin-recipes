Satay

Description

Sate (Also spelled satay)

Satay is a popular Southeast Asian dish consisting of seasoned, skewered, and grilled meat, often served with a rich peanut sauce. Originating in Indonesia, satay is now enjoyed in many countries, including Malaysia, Thailand, and Singapore. The meat—commonly chicken, beef, lamb, or goat—is marinated in a mixture of spices and herbs, then grilled over charcoal to give it a smoky, flavorful aroma. Satay is typically served with rice cakes (called lontong or ketupat), sliced cucumbers, onions, and a savory peanut sauce that adds a creamy, slightly sweet, and spicy flavor. It is a beloved street food and a favorite dish during festivals and gatherings.

Indonesia is home to a wide variety of satay, each region offering its own unique twist on this beloved dish. From Sabang to Merauke, satay (or sate, in Indonesian) is more than just skewered meat—it reflects the rich cultural and culinary diversity of the archipelago. There are over 30 known regional variations of satay, each differing in the choice of meat, marinades, sauces, and cooking techniques.

Some of the most famous types include Sate Madura, known for its sweet and savory peanut sauce; Sate Padang, with its thick, spicy yellow sauce made from turmeric and rice flour; and Sate Lilit from Bali, where minced meat is mixed with grated coconut and wrapped around lemongrass sticks instead of skewers. There's also Sate Maranggi from West Java, which is marinated in a blend of vinegar and spices, and Sate Klatak from Yogyakarta, unique for being grilled on iron skewers with a simple salt and garlic seasoning.

These diverse types of satay are often influenced by local ingredients, traditions, and even religious practices, making satay not only a popular street food but also a symbol of Indonesia's rich culinary heritage.

As there are many types of satay, I'll now share the recipe for one variation called Madura satay.

Preparation Time: 180 minutes

Yield: 1 kodi (20 skewers)

Ingredients :

  • 250 grams chicken fillet
  • 5 kaffir lime leaves
  • 250 grams peanuts
  • 5 tablespoons sweet soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons palm sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Water, as needed
  • Cooking oil, for sautéing

Marinade Ingredients:

  • 2 teaspoons coriander seeds
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • Sweet soy sauce, to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Ground Spice Paste:

  • 5 curly red chilies
  • 8 shallots
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 5 candlenuts

How to Make (Steps) :

  1. Cut the chicken fillet into small cubes.
  2. Skewer the chicken pieces onto bamboo satay sticks.
  3. Grind the marinade ingredients until smooth.
  4. Marinate the skewered chicken with the marinade mixture and let it rest.
  5. Roast or fry the peanuts and kaffir lime leaves until golden brown and fragrant
  6. Let the peanuts cool, then grind them coarsely using a food chopper, blender, or mortar and pestle.
  7. Sauté the ground spice paste until fragrant.
  8. Add the ground peanuts and stir well.
  9. Add sweet soy sauce, sugar, and salt.
  10. Pour in enough water to adjust the consistency.
  11. Cook the sauce until it thickens and the oil separates.
  12. Coat the chicken satay with the marinade again before grilling.
  13. Grill the marinated satay over charcoal.
  14. Baste with the leftover marinade while grilling.
  15. Turn the skewers regularly to ensure even cooking.
  16. Serve the grilled satay with the Madura-style peanut sauce.
